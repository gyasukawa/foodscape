Frequently Asked Questions

What's a foodscape?

A foodscape is a place where food is produced or could be produced. Vegetable gardens, small farms, fruit trees, herb beds, backyard chickens, honey combs are all great examples of foodscapes!

Do foodscapes use organic seeds/plants?

Each foodscape is created by the host, so read the foodscape profile to learn more about their growing practices. Some foodscapes may not be certified organic, even if they are not using any chemical pesticides. You can always contact the foodscape host to learn more about their food.

I grow food! Why should I use Foodscape?

Ever have handfuls of extra basil? Or tons of extra zucchini? Foodscape provides an easy way for you to share your backyard bounty with your friends, neighbors, and community, so that none of your produce goes to waste. Plus, you can use any extra money from your contributors to buy things for your foodscape... like more plants, seeds, fancy garden gloves, or harvest baskets.

What do foodscape contributors receive?

Contributors receive whatever the foodscape host says is available to contributors. You can share part of the harvest, tours of your foodscape, and/or other treats to your contributors.

I do not have a foodscape, but want to support local, homegrown food. Can I use Foodscape?

Yes! You can support a local foodscape in your area.
